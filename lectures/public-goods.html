Classification of goods

We can divide goods into four groups based on two definitions:

  • Rivalrous Goods are goods whose consumption means they cannot be consumed by others. Examples: food, almost all material goods. Examples of nonrivalrous goods include: public health, quiet, works of art, national defense.

  • Excludable Goods are goods for which the supplier can limit access to only those who pay for it. Examples: Digital movies, almost all material goods. Examples of Nonexcludable goods inlcude clean air, clean water, public pastures.

Classifying types of goods

Markets efficiently provide only the first category of goods.

Why Can’t Markets Provide Other Types of Goods?

  • Goods that are nonexcludable suffer from a “free-rider” problem. Example: group projects.
  • Goods that are nonrivalrous, the most efficient price is zero.

Public Goods

Public goods are both nonexcludable and nonrivalrous. In this way they are the opposite of private goods.

Examples:

  • National Defense
  • Scientific discoveries
  • Herd immunity

The marginal social benefit of a public good is the sum of the individual marginal benefits.

Common Pool Resources

Common Pool Resources

A common pool resource is rivalrous but nonexcludable.

The issue is one of overuse. How do we get individuals to take into account the effect of their actions on the group?

  • Tax or quota limits.
  • Make the resource private.
  • Tradable permits to use the resource.
  • Community agreement to limit use.

Community Solutions to Resource Management

“New Institutional Economics” pioneered by Nobel Laureate Elinor Ostrom.

Top-down solutions like taxes and permits are subject to issues:

  • corruption
  • difficulty monitoring

Communities can manage themselves if group cohesion is high:

  • members share similar viewpoints and goals and are relatively equal.
  • other social interactions create bonds that encourage participation.

Artificially Scarce Goods

Artificially scare goods are excludable but nonrivalrous. These are things like digital media.

  • Excludable so sellers charge a positive price,
  • but nonrivalrous so more people could consume them and increase total social benefit.

Solution is similar to natural monopoly: regulate price (or just accept inefficiency).
