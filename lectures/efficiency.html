Total Surplus

  • Total Surplus - Total Surplus is the value to both consumers and producers of all the transactions in a market. It is the sum of producer surplus and consumer surplus.

Market Interventions

The government could:

  • Reallocate between consumers - redistribute goods from a consumer who bought it to a consumer who didnt.
  • Reallocate between sellers - redistribute goods from a producers who sells to a producer who doesnt.
  • Change the quantity traded - set a limit on the quantity sold.

When Markets Work Well

  • Lots of producers and consumers (not enough sellers leads to monopoly).
  • Well defined property rights (who owns what means of production, what income, etc).
  • Enough information about the goods being sold (uncertainty makes purchasing difficult).

When Markets Dont Work Well

  • Externalities - If a market transaction leads to an externality (i.e.production damages others via pollution).
  • Poorly defined property rights - Its hard to sell or buy when theres uncertainty about ownership.
  • Monopoly or Oligopoly - When there arent enough sellers, monopoly producers can charge excess prices.
  • Monopsony - When there are only a few buyers, they can withold transcations to force producers to sell for low prices.
  • Thinness - If there are not many transactions, (i.e.the market is ‘thin’) then there are opportunities for arbitrage.
