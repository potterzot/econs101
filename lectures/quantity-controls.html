Catching up on price controls

  • Price Floor - A minimum price at which a product can be sold. Typically this is applied by purchasing excess supply (driving up the price to the set price floor).

Effect of a price floor

Effect of a price floor

Why a Price Floor?

  • Society values an industry and is willing to pay to support it even if it is not profitable.
  • An industry is politically powerful and can lobby for price supports.

Quotas

  • Quantity Controls / Quotas - A Quota is a government limit on the amount of a product that can be produced. This is typically done by allowing only licensed producers to produce, and in limited amount.

E.g., licenses to operate a business do the same thing. Liquor licenses, bar licenses, doctor and lawyer licensing, etc

Effect of a quota

Why a Quota?

  • Protect renewable resources from over-extraction.
  • Limit imports from other countries or from competing industries.
  • Restrict competition from other firms.
