Taxes can be…

  • Progressive tax - A tax that increases the percentage paid as income increases.
  • Regressive tax - A tax that decreases the percentage paid as income increases.
  • Proportional tax - A tax that is the same percentage of income regardless of income level.

Subsidies / Tax Breaks

  • A subsidy is a negative tax in which the government gives money.

Examples: Farm subsidies, corporate subsidies

Subsidies / Tax Breaks: Competition over companies

Cities often compete to incentivize a company to move there by giving significant tax breaks. Often the cost of these tax breaks is higher than the benefit of the company.

Source

Subsidies / Tax Breaks: Support for low-income families

  • The earned income tax credit (EITC) is a negative income tax for families with low incomes ($6000 - $15000 for no children, $10000-$40000 for one child).
  • An interesting proposal much discussed globally is the Universal Basic Income (UBI), in which all residents receive a subsidy, regardless of their income.
