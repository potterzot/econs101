Aggregate Demand

  • Aggregate demand - The sum of all consumer demand. Demand curves are summed horizontally.

Substitutes and complements

  • Substitutes - Two goods are substitutes if a decrease in the price of one good decreases demand for the other good.

Examples include food away from home (FAFH) and food at home (FAH).

  • Complements - Two goods are complements if a decrease in the price of one good increases demand for the other good.

Examples include gas prices and milk.

Normal and inferior goods

  • Normal goods - A normal good is one in which an increase in income increases demand for that good or vice-versa.

Examples include pretty much all goods.

  • Inferior goods - Goods are inferior if an increase in income decreases demand for that good or vice-versa.
